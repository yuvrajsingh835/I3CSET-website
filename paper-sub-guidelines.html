Submission Guidelines

Everything you need to know for your I3CSET-2026 contribution

Note: Proposals for Special Sessions/Tracks are warmly invited. Please contact the organizing committee for early expressions of interest.

Submission Process

All papers must be submitted electronically through Microsoft CMT. Submissions must include necessary details such as paper ID and title, author names, affiliations, and contact information.

Submit via Microsoft CMT

Originality and Scope

All submitted manuscripts must be original, unpublished, and not under consideration for publication elsewhere. Manuscripts should present significant and novel contributions to the field relevant to the conference themes.

Plagiarism exceeding 20% (iThenticate) may lead to immediate rejection.

Manuscript Length and Format

  • The maximum manuscript length is 5 pages, including figures, tables, and references. Additional pages may be included for an extra fee.
  • Authors must prepare their manuscripts according to the Taylor & Francis conference template, which can be downloaded below.
  • Manuscripts must be formatted in English, using clear and concise language.

Camera-Ready Submission

Upon acceptance, authors must submit a camera-ready version of their manuscript by the specified deadline, incorporating reviewers’ comments and strictly following the Taylor & Francis formatting requirements.

Presentation Requirement

At least one author must register for the conference and present the paper (in person or virtually). Failure to present may lead to exclusion of the paper from the proceedings.

Publication

Accepted papers will be published in the Taylor & Francis conference proceedings series and submitted for indexing in Scopus and Web of Science.

Ethical Considerations

  • Authors must adhere to ethical guidelines, including proper citation of prior work and avoiding duplicate publication.
  • Any conflicts of interest must be disclosed during the submission process.
